What Yale Could Do With Just 1% of Its Endowment

Yale's endowment is $40.7 billion. Just 1% of that is $407 million. Here's what Yale could do with that money instead of hoarding it:

Give every Yale worker a $50,000 bonus ($400 million)

Yale has approximately 8,000 employees. A $50,000 bonus for each worker would cost $400 million. This would be life-changing money for workers struggling with housing costs, student debt, and rising inflation.

Build 1,000 affordable housing units for workers ($400 million)

At $400,000 per unit, Yale could build 1,000 affordable housing units for workers struggling with New Haven's high rent costs. This would provide stable, affordable housing for workers and their families.

Provide free childcare for all Yale workers ($300 million)

Yale could provide free, high-quality childcare for all workers with children. This would cost approximately $300 million annually, making it easier for workers to balance family and work responsibilities.

What Yale Could Do With 5% of Its Endowment

Pay every Yale worker $100,000 per year minimum ($2 billion)

Yale could guarantee every worker a minimum salary of $100,000 per year. This would cost approximately $2 billion annually, or 5% of the endowment. Workers would no longer struggle to make ends meet in one of the most expensive cities in America.

Build 5,000 affordable housing units ($2 billion)

Yale could build 5,000 affordable housing units for workers and community members. This would transform New Haven's housing crisis and provide stable homes for thousands of families.

The Reality: What Yale Actually Does

Instead of using its wealth to help workers, Yale:

  • Fights union organizing efforts with expensive lawyers
  • Pays administrators millions while workers struggle
  • Invests in hedge funds and private equity
  • Refuses to pay workers a living wage
  • Lobbies against worker-friendly policies

Yale's endowment grew by $1.4 billion in 2023 alone. That's enough money to give every Yale worker a $175,000 bonus. Instead, workers are told there's "no money" for raises, benefits, or better working conditions.

Yale spends more on investment management fees than it does on worker salaries. The university pays hundreds of millions to Wall Street firms while workers struggle with housing, healthcare, and childcare costs.

Yale's endowment could pay every worker $100,000 per year for the next 50 years and still have $20 billion left. The university chooses to hoard wealth instead of investing in its workers.

Workers Deserve Better

Yale workers keep the university running. They clean the buildings, serve the food, maintain the facilities, process the paperwork, and provide essential services. They deserve:

  • Living wages that keep up with inflation
  • Affordable healthcare and childcare
  • Safe and dignified working conditions
  • Respect and recognition for their contributions
  • A voice in decisions that affect their lives

Yale has the money. Yale has the power. Yale has the responsibility. It's time for Yale to invest in its workers instead of hoarding wealth for the wealthy.

Yale vs. Public Universities

While Yale hoards $40.7 billion, public universities face devastating budget cuts. State funding for higher education has been slashed by $9 billion since 2008, forcing tuition increases and program cuts that hurt working-class students.

Yale's endowment could restore all state funding cuts to public universities. The $9 billion in cuts to public higher education could be fully restored with just 22% of Yale's endowment. Yale could do this and still have $31.7 billion left.

Yale's endowment could provide free tuition at every public university for 2 years. The total cost of tuition at all public universities is about $20 billion annually. Yale's endowment could make public higher education free for 2 years and still have $700 million remaining.

Yale's endowment could fund the entire community college system for 4 years. The annual cost of community college education nationwide is about $10 billion. Yale's endowment could fund free community college for 4 years and still have $700 million left.

Yale's endowment could eliminate tuition at every HBCU for 10 years. The total annual tuition revenue at all Historically Black Colleges and Universities is about $4 billion. Yale's endowment could make HBCUs tuition-free for 10 years and still have $700 million remaining.

Federal Tax Comparisons

Yale's endowment is larger than the annual budget of 15 U.S. states combined. The university pays virtually no taxes on its $40.7 billion endowment, while working families pay taxes on every dollar they earn.

Yale's endowment could pay the entire federal education budget for 2 years. The Department of Education's annual budget is about $20 billion. Yale's endowment could fund all federal education programs for 2 years and still have $700 million left.

Yale's endowment is 8 times larger than the entire federal budget for student financial aid. The federal government spends about $5 billion annually on Pell Grants and other student aid. Yale's endowment could fund this program for 8 years and still have $700 million remaining.

Yale's endowment could eliminate all student debt for 2 million Americans. With $40.7 billion, Yale could pay off the average student debt ($20,000) for 2 million people and still have $700 million remaining.
